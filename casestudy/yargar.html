Case Study: Yargar

Managing and supervising a fair distribution of agricultural inputs.

Department of Agriculture of Golestan Province

  • Industry: Agricultural
  • Size: 20,000+
  • Website: yargar.ir
The Agricultural Organization of Golestan Province and its related organizations needed a comprehensive software solution to manage and monitor the distribution of agricultural inputs.

Project Requirements

  • Tracking agricultural inputs
  • Management of quotas, distributors and factories
  • Allocation based on defined procedures
  • Creating communication between relevant organizations
  • Prevent smuggling

Project Overview

Golestan province with more than 170,000 farmers and 40,000 livestock farmers is one of the top 3 provinces in Iran's agriculture and animal husbandry sectors. Considering that agricultural inputs in Iran include government subsidies, there are many challenges in their distribution.

Yargar is a comprehensive system for managing and monitoring the distribution of agricultural inputs. With Yargar, inputs can be tracked from the time they are allocated to a province until they reach the end user. The primary goal of Yargar is to prevent the smuggling of agricultural inputs.

The Challenge

Long queues, uncertainty of input delivery, denial of receiving inputs and re-requesting, inefficient authentication, and as a result the growth of brokers and the creation of a secondary market and smuggling. The time-consuming preparation of reports for high-level managers and the unreliability of obtained reports, along with the creation of internal corruption, were among the challenges of agricultural input distribution.

The Approach & Solution

To track agricultural inputs effectively, we must record all activities in Yargar. The process works as follows: The inputs that enter the province are registered by the person in charge of the organization and distributed among the cities of the province. Then the managers of each city divide the obtained quota among the legal distributors defined in the Yargar system.

Finally, based on the procedures already defined in Yargar, a quota is generated for users who are authenticated in the Yargar system, and the amount of this quota varies according to each user's needs. Then, users can receive their input through online or in-person requests by presenting the confirmation code that has been sent to their mobile phones.

The Result

Efficiency

600%

Improved the distribution of inputs by 6 times.

Customer Satisfaction

95%

High percentage of satisfaction among Yargar users.

Distribution Volume

+500,000T

Distribution of more than 500 thousand tons of inputs.

Environmental Impact

+60%

Up to 60 percent reduction in fuel and paper consumption.

By using the Yargar system in the distribution of agricultural and livestock inputs, not only were many problems solved regarding fair distribution, but also the reports produced by Yargar can be of great help for future decisions of the province in the direction of better production of agricultural and livestock products. Furthermore, the possibility of connecting to weather forecasting systems can help provide timely and targeted weather warnings. One of the most important results of Yargar is a 60% reduction in paper consumption for administrative affairs and inter-organizational communication and introduction letters. It also resulted in lower fuel consumption thanks to reduced vehicle traffic throughout the province.
